Barbara — ease, light, and a lot of laughing

A model and actress who's been performing since she was a child — and it shows. With Barbara Cia the session was equal parts craft and play.

Some models you have to guide through every frame. Barbara isn’t one of them. She’s been performing since she was a child — opera and theatre first, then years of fashion and commercial work in London — and that history is written into the way she moves. She arrived prepared, warmed up in about five minutes, and from there it stopped feeling like photographer and model and started feeling like two people building images together.

What strikes you first is the professionalism — punctual, easy about the plan, completely at home in front of the lens, reading the light and adjusting before I even asked. She’s an art director by training too, so she brings ideas, not just poses. What stays with you afterwards is how much fun it was: we laughed our way through half the setups, and the best frames came out of exactly those moments — the ones where nobody was trying to pose at all.

That’s the thing this work depends on and can’t fake — trust, and a bit of joy in the room. Barbara brought both. These are a few of my favourites from the day.

It never felt like posing. We just kept trying things, laughing when they didn't work, and every so often something clicked and we both knew it.

— Barbara
